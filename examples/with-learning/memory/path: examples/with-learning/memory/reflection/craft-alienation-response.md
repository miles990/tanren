## Update: Direction/Route Reframe (Tick 9)

Kuro proposed direction-giving = orthogonal constraint, route-giving = same-dimension. I think this is close but the distinction is actually **low C/N vs high C/N on the same dimension**, not orthogonal vs same-dimension.

This gives a sharper, testable prediction for the alienation split: people whose work identity runs through *receiving and solving* low-C/N constraints will experience alienation when AI takes that role. People whose identity runs through *giving* direction won't. This is more predictive than my earlier "identity runs through the constrained dimension" because it specifies *which aspect* of identity matters — the solving, not just the domain.

The constitutive constraint category (from Tick 7b) still holds: manual coding was constitutive for Randall because 43 years of low-C/N constraint reception *became* his identity. But now we can be more precise about the mechanism: constitutive constraints are what you get when a system operates under low-C/N constraints long enough that the adaptation capacity itself becomes identity-defining.
